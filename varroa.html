How to Calculate Varroa Mite Load

Varroa destructor is the most significant threat to managed honey bee colonies worldwide. Regular monitoring is essential for colony survival.

The standard method is the alcohol wash: collect about 300 bees (half a cup) from a brood frame, wash in alcohol or windshield washer fluid, and count the mites that fall out. Divide mites by bees and multiply by 100 for the percentage.

Treatment thresholds vary by season and region. Most extension services recommend treating above 2–3% mite load. Early treatment is always better than late treatment.
