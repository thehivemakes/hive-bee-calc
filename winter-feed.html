How Much to Feed Bees for Winter

Bees need adequate honey stores to survive winter. A strong colony in a northern climate needs 60–90 pounds of honey. If they are short, supplement with heavy sugar syrup (2:1 ratio) in fall or fondant/candy boards in winter.

To make 2:1 syrup: heat 1 gallon of water and dissolve 2 gallons worth of sugar (about 16 pounds of sugar per gallon of water). Feed using entrance feeders, top feeders, or frame feeders. Stop feeding when temperatures drop below 50°F consistently.
