Planning Around the Nectar Flow

The nectar flow is the period when major nectar sources are blooming and bees are actively foraging and producing surplus honey. Timing your management around the flow is critical for a successful harvest.

Add honey supers before the flow starts so bees have space to store nectar. A colony that runs out of space may swarm or backfill the brood nest. Check supers weekly during peak flow and add more when 2/3 full.
